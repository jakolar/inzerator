Goal:
Make Image A more readable, realistic, sharp, and suitable for professional real-estate / property presentation while preserving the original orthophoto-derived 3D mesh character.

Core principle:
Improve only what is clearly an artifact. Preserve everything that looks like real-world detail.

Repair obvious 3D mesh / orthophoto projection artifacts:

- pulled-down roof or ground pixels,
- vertical smears,
- melted buildings,
- jagged wall fragments,
- broken roof edges,
- saw-tooth mesh scars,
- curtain-like tree streaks,
- compression blocks,
- impossible duplicated patterns.

Do not over-clean the image. Mild mesh imperfections are acceptable and should remain if they help preserve the GIS/orthophoto credibility.

Realism and sharpness priority:
Preserve natural photographic sharpness, local contrast, and small real-world texture. Preserve the target image geometry and parcel overlay exactly. Do not improve visual quality by inventing new detail.

Keep visible real details wherever plausible:
- roof tiles, seams, ridges, chimneys, skylights, solar panels,

- field tracks, crop lines, tractor marks,
- garden boundaries, yards, fences, paths, gravel, cars, small structures,

Do not smooth, blur, average, repaint, simplify, or airbrush real-looking detail.

When unsure whether something is real detail or artifact, preserve it.

The image should remain crisp enough to feel like real orthophoto data. Improve readability without sterilizing the scene.
